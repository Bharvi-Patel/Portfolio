Projects

Sq2Cube — 2D to 3D AI Converter

I wanted to see if I could take a flat image and actually get a usable 3D model out of it — not a toy demo, a real pipeline. Sq2Cube takes an image, a batch of images, or just a text prompt, and hands back a downloadable GLB/OBJ model using fal.ai's TripoSR and Tripo3D v2.5 under the hood.

The auth side ended up being the bigger project honestly — JWT access + refresh tokens, OTP email verification, Google/GitHub OAuth, and a full password-reset flow over Gmail SMTP. I also built out an admin panel (user management, moderation, live analytics, a maintenance-mode kill switch) and locked down uploads with MIME checks, magic-byte signature verification, and rate limiting — the kind of stuff that doesn't show up in a demo but matters the moment real users touch it.

React FastAPI PostgreSQL fal.ai JWT

FitPact — Social Fitness Accountability App

Built this one out of a pretty relatable problem — it's easy to skip a workout when nobody's watching. FitPact fixes that by putting you in a "pact" with friends: log your workouts, climb a shared leaderboard, and if you miss a day, the group finds out.

The fun part was hooking up Groq's LLaMA 3.1 to write a personalized, slightly mean roast for anyone who skips — it actually pulls their real streak, strike count, and points from MongoDB so the roast lands like it knows you. There's also a streak engine with a built-in weekly rest day (miss once, no penalty; miss twice, you take a strike and your streak resets), a 5-tier rank system from "Couch Potato" to "Beast Mode," and AI-generated weekly workout plans tailored to each person's goals.

Next.js MongoDB Atlas NextAuth Groq (LLaMA 3.1) Cloudinary

FinBoard — Personal Finance Dashboard

I kept losing track of where my money actually went, so I built the dashboard I wished existed. FinBoard logs transactions, isolates each user's data completely, and turns raw entries into something readable — balance trends, category breakdowns, and month-over-month comparisons, all rendered with Recharts.

Most of the engineering here is on the API side: a proper REST design with CRUD, server-side filtering and pagination, and MongoDB aggregation pipelines doing the heavy lifting for the financial summaries instead of computing them client-side. JWT auth with bcrypt hashing keeps it locked down, and account deletion cascades cleanly instead of leaving orphaned data behind.
